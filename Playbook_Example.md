# Example: Pattern 1 – Community Anchor Point

## 0. Header Card
- **Pattern:** Pattern 1: Community Anchor Point
- **Pattern Tagline:** A welcoming, visible, multipurpose place where everything connects
- **Sprint window:** January 15 → April 15, 2025
- **Level this sprint:** ☑ Basic only
- **Primary Wicked Problems:** WP4 Isolation & Disconnection, WP10 Underused Assets, WP13 Civic Apathy

## 1. Pattern Purpose & Local Fit
**1.1 Pattern problem (localized)**
- Pattern Problem: Towns lack a "home base" so fragmentation, isolation, and duplicated effort pile up
- Local re-write: "In Milltown, we have a beautiful old library building that sits empty most days. Meanwhile, three different groups are trying to organize community events but don't know about each other. We need one visible place where everything connects."

**1.2 What success looks like in 90 days**
- Library open 6 hours/week with consistent host
- 3 different groups using the space regularly
- Monthly calendar posted in 5 visible locations
- 50+ residents know this is "the place to go"

**1.3 Wicked Problems we're targeting**
- WP4: People feel isolated; no regular gathering rhythm
- WP10: Beautiful library building underused
- WP13: Civic energy scattered; no visible center

## 2. Pattern Stack
**2.1 Primary pattern:** Pattern 1: Community Anchor Point

**2.2 Supporting patterns:**
- Pattern 7 (Village Broadcast Loop) – to advertise what's happening at the anchor
- Pattern 2 (Shared Cultural Story) – to capture stories from the space

## 3. Roles & Stewardship
**3.1 Stewardship roles**
- Pattern Steward: Maria Chen, Library Director
- Host organization: Milltown Public Library
- Stewardship circle: Maria (library), Tom (city council), Sarah (historical society), James (youth center), Linda (senior center)

**3.2 Partners needed**
- City (building access, utilities)
- School district (after-school programs)
- Local businesses (coffee donations, printing)

## 4. 90-Day Rhythm
[Use standard template above]

## 5. Phase Plan
**Phase 0 – Setup**
- Confirm library as anchor point
- Form 5-person steward team
- Get keys, Wi-Fi password, room-use guidelines

**Phase 1 – Weeks 1–4**
- Week 1: Open 2 hours Saturday morning, coffee + conversation
- Week 2: Host historical society meeting
- Week 3: Youth homework club試試
- Week 4: Community potluck + mini-retro

**Phase 2 – Weeks 5–8**
- Add Tuesday evening hours
- Create simple room-use guidelines
- Activate unused back room for small meetings

**Phase 3 – Weeks 9–12**
- Capture 3 resident stories about the space
- Document what worked/didn't
- Decide: keep going or add Advanced features (formal MOUs, paid coordinator)

## 6. Communications & Story Layer
**6.1 Broadcast loop**
- Primary: Printed one-pager at post office, café, clinic
- Cadence: Weekly
- Editor: Sarah (historical society)

**6.2 Story capture**
- Method: Photos + short quotes
- Where: Bulletin board in library, monthly newsletter

## 7. Measures & Signals
**7.1 Core signals**
- Participation: 50+ unique visitors in 90 days
- Diversity: Youth, working adults, seniors all represented
- Collaboration: 3+ orgs using space who didn't before

**7.2 Pattern-specific signals**
- Hours/week doors open: target 6
- Events hosted: target 12
- Repeat visitors: target 30%

## 8. Level-Up Path
**Evolved Idea:** Anchor becomes coordinated hub with paid part-time coordinator, formal partnerships, shared calendar system

**We will consider leveling up when:**
- Demand exceeds 6 hours/week capacity
- Multiple groups want regular access
- Funding identified for coordinator role

**If we level up, we add:**
- Part-time coordinator (10 hrs/week)
- MOUs with city, school, nonprofits
- Online booking system

## 9. Artifacts to Keep
- Sprint Card with 12 weeks of logs
- One-pager: "What We Learned"
- 3 resident stories + photos
- Room-use guidelines (draft)
- List of 5 stewards + 8 partner contacts
